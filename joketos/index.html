0. Preamble to Humor

WHEREAS, the Provider (hereinafter referred to as "me," "myself," "the individual responsible for these words," or "the comedy genius you don't deserve") has determined that legal documents are insufferably boring, unnecessarily complex, and deliberately designed to discourage actual reading; and

WHEREAS, the User (hereinafter referred to as "you," "the poor soul reading this," "person with questionable time-management skills," or "individual who has clearly exhausted all other entertainment options including watching paint dry") has somehow found themselves reading a joke terms of service document when they could be engaging in literally any other activity; and

WHEREAS, both parties acknowledge that life is too short for actual legalese but just long enough for parodied legalese that serves no practical purpose whatsoever except to amuse and/or confuse;

WHEREAS, the Provider has taken it upon themselves to create this unnecessarily verbose document despite having no legal training whatsoever beyond watching courtroom dramas and occasionally arguing with strangers on the internet;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, though neither party is actually receiving anything of value except perhaps a mild chuckle, the parties agree as follows:

1. Definitions

For the purposes of this document, which you are likely not reading unless you're legally bored, pathologically curious, or just nosy, the following definitions shall apply with excessive specificity and unnecessary elaboration:

"Jokes": Any arrangement of words, gestures, memes, or comedic phenomena intended to elicit laughter, chuckles, snorts, nose-exhales, or catastrophic silence. Includes, but is not limited to, puns, absurdities, ironies, and occasional existential dread masked in humor. May also include statements that were not intended to be funny but were received as such due to poor delivery, cultural misunderstandings, or the general absurdity of human existence.

"Provider": The legally unqualified, socially questionable source of aforementioned Jokes—may also be referred to as "me" in less formal contracts or DMs. The Provider makes no claims of comedic expertise, originality, or good taste, and acknowledges that many of their Jokes were likely overheard at parties, subconsciously absorbed from sitcoms, or dreamed up during particularly boring meetings.

"User": The unlucky individual who reads, hears, or is exposed to the Jokes. This includes scrollers, lurkers, followers, enemies, people who laugh just to be polite, individuals who happened to be in the wrong place at the wrong time, and those who mistakenly thought they were clicking on something else entirely. The User is presumed to have at least a rudimentary sense of humor, though this presumption is rebuttable upon presentation of evidence to the contrary.

"Engagement": Any act of attention, response, or passive absorption of the Jokes, whether voluntary, accidental, coerced, or under peer pressure. Engagement may range from active participation (e.g., laughing, sharing, responding) to passive acknowledgment (e.g., slight smile, internal eye-roll, existential questioning of how you ended up here reading this).

"Dark Humor": Material designed to poke at life's darker corners—mortality, societal hypocrisy, or whatever Freud would've side-eyed. May or may not cause laughter followed by guilt, followed by laughter again, followed by checking over your shoulder to ensure no one witnessed your momentary moral lapse. Dark humor is like electricity—most effective when properly grounded.

"Dad Joke": A form of humor characterized by its predictability, simplicity, and ability to make teenagers roll their eyes so hard they can see their own brains. Often preceded by "Hi Hungry, I'm Dad." Dad jokes are the comedic equivalent of wearing socks with sandals—objectively terrible yet somehow endearing in their commitment to uncoolness.

"Meme": A digital artifact of questionable origin and even more questionable taste that somehow manages to encapsulate the collective consciousness of an entire generation using nothing but Impact font and a stock photo. Memes are the hieroglyphics of modern society, destined to confuse future archaeologists who will undoubtedly write doctoral theses attempting to explain why a picture of a distressed-looking woman yelling at a confused cat was considered the pinnacle of human communication.

"Pun": The lowest form of humor according to people who aren't clever enough to make them. A linguistic play on words that causes simultaneous groaning and admiration. Puns are the comedic equivalent of a surprise tax audit—nobody claims to want them, yet they persist with remarkable tenacity throughout human history.

"Sarcasm": A sophisticated form of wit in which the speaker says the opposite of what they mean, typically delivered with a distinctive tone that somehow still manages to be missed by approximately 37% of the population. Sarcasm is the unofficial language of the chronically disappointed and the perpetually unimpressed. In written form, it is frequently misinterpreted, leading to unnecessary arguments, clarifications, and the invention of the "/s" tag, which ruins the joke but prevents digital bloodshed.

"Satire": The art of making fun of something so accurately that people who don't get it think you're serious, and people who do get it worry about those who don't. Satire is humor's equivalent of a Trojan horse—it sneaks important commentary past your defenses by disguising itself as mere entertainment.

If you're still reading these definitions, you might be a lawyer, someone with an impressive attention to detail, or someone who genuinely enjoys reading instruction manuals for fun. We don't judge. Well, we do, but we're keeping those judgments to ourselves for now.

2. Dark Humor Disclaimer

By proceeding to consume, acknowledge, or mentally replay any Jokes classified or misclassified as "Dark Humor," the User agrees that some content may be considered wildly inappropriate in polite company, most funerals, or HR training. The Provider is not responsible for emotional, psychological, or cosmological crises induced by such content. If you're crying and laughing at the same time, you're welcome.

Dark humor, like medicine, should be administered with care and only to those with sufficient psychological fortitude. Side effects may include uncomfortable truths, sudden realizations about mortality, and the urge to share inappropriately timed jokes at family gatherings. If you experience a moral crisis lasting more than four hours, please consult your local philosopher or therapist.

The Provider maintains that dark humor is like food in a famine—not everyone gets it. This is not to make light of famines, which are serious humanitarian crises, but rather to illustrate through inappropriately dark metaphor the very nature of dark humor itself, thus creating a self-referential example of the concept being explained, which is either clever meta-commentary or just lazy writing, depending on your perspective and how much coffee the Provider had consumed at the time of drafting.

Dark humor serves a vital psychological function in helping humans process the inherent absurdity and occasional horror of existence. Without it, we would be forced to confront the meaninglessness of life directly, which would significantly reduce productivity and almost certainly increase the sales of both ice cream and existentialist literature.

7. User Conduct

  • User shall not dissect the Jokes in a manner that renders them clinically unfunny. This includes Reddit threads and philosophy majors. The Provider acknowledges that humor, like a frog, dies when dissected, no matter how educational the process might be.
  • Complaints about taste, tone, or timing must be sent to the void and will be acknowledged by the nearest raccoon. The Provider maintains a strict policy of selective hearing when it comes to criticism, a strategy developed through years of family holiday dinners and performance reviews.
  • Users agree that bad jokes are part of the ecosystem and shall be treated like rain: inconvenient but unavoidable. Without bad jokes, we would have no metric by which to measure good jokes, much like how one cannot truly appreciate daylight without having experienced darkness, or how one cannot truly appreciate good health without having experienced that time you ate gas station sushi.
  • Users shall not attempt to explain the Jokes to those who didn't get them the first time. Nothing kills humor faster than explanation, except perhaps a PowerPoint presentation about humor complete with pie charts analyzing punchline efficiency and bar graphs measuring audience response rates across demographic segments.
  • The practice of saying "no offense" before delivering clearly offensive Jokes does not exempt the User from social consequences, nor does it magically transform inappropriate content into acceptable banter. Provider commits to deploying humor with some vague degree of moral compass. While lines may be tiptoed, hopped over, or occasionally cartwheeled across, content is not intended to target vulnerable communities, spread hate, or summon ancient gods who have been peacefully slumbering in the depths of the ocean and would be quite cranky if awakened.

Should any Joke be perceived as irreparably offensive, the Provider will perform a ritual of self-reflection (approx. 0.6 seconds), then carry on with life—possibly wiser, possibly not. The Provider acknowledges that growth is a non-linear process with occasional backsliding, particularly when a really good joke opportunity presents itself.

The Provider acknowledges that humor evolves over time, and what was once considered acceptable comedy may later be recognized as problematic. The Provider reserves the right to grow, learn, and occasionally cringe at their past attempts at humor, much like you do when Facebook shows you "memories" from 2011 that make you question every life choice that led to that particular status update.

In the event that a Joke crosses a moral boundary, the Provider may issue a half-hearted apology consisting primarily of the phrases "if anyone was offended" and "that wasn't my intention," followed by absolutely no change in behavior whatsoever. The Provider acknowledges that this is not ideal but claims it is "authentic" and "on-brand."

The Provider maintains that comedy should "punch up" rather than "punch down," a principle that sounds noble but is conveniently flexible depending on one's perspective on the social hierarchy. The Provider reserves the right to determine what constitutes "up" and "down" based on whatever justification allows them to make the joke they really want to make.

9. Intellectual Property Rights

All Jokes, whether brilliant, mediocre, or groan-inducing, remain the intellectual property of the Provider, or whoever the Provider stole them from originally. The Provider makes no claims of originality, as humor is merely the recycling of human experience through the lens of absurdity, much like how all pop songs are just variations on the same four chords but with different hairstyles.

Users are granted a non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license to remember, retell, and repurpose these Jokes, provided that:

  • When retelling a Joke that receives significant laughter, the User must mumble "I heard that somewhere" to avoid appearing more clever than they actually are. This maintains the cosmic balance of unearned credit and prevents the User from developing an inflated sense of their own wit.
  • When retelling a Joke that bombs catastrophically, the User must loudly proclaim "That's one of Kai's jokes" to properly attribute blame. The Provider accepts this burden as part of their comedic journey and appreciates the opportunity to learn from distant failures without having to be physically present for the awkward silence.
  • Any modifications, adaptations, or improvements to the original Jokes must be shared with the Provider so they can claim them as their own in future social gatherings. This is the comedy equivalent of open-source software, except with none of the ethical principles or community benefits.
  • In the event that a User's retelling of a Joke results in actual professional comedy opportunities, the Provider is entitled to 10% of all proceeds, payable in either legal tender or baked goods of equivalent value. This clause is entirely unenforceable but makes the Provider feel better about giving away content for free.

The Provider acknowledges that all humor is derivative and probably dates back to some prehistoric cave painting that depicted a mammoth slipping on a banana peel. Originality in comedy, much like in literature, music, and cave painting, is largely an illusion maintained by our collective agreement to pretend we haven't heard that one before.

10. Force Majeure

The Provider shall not be held responsible for failure to deliver humor during acts of God, natural disasters, political upheavals, global pandemics, Mercury retrograde, or particularly bad hair days. Some events are simply beyond the scope of comedy, though the Provider acknowledges that historically, some of the best humor has emerged from the darkest times, proving that humans will find a way to laugh even when it seems least appropriate.

In the event of a zombie apocalypse, alien invasion, or the rise of sentient AI, all terms of this agreement shall be suspended, as survival will take precedence over comedy. However, the Provider reserves the right to make inappropriate jokes even during such calamities, as gallows humor is a recognized coping mechanism and may be one of the few things distinguishing humans from either zombies, aliens, or artificial intelligence, depending on which apocalyptic scenario unfolds.

The Provider's ability to be funny may be temporarily impaired by personal circumstances including but not limited to: heartbreak, existential crisis, hangovers, or binge-watching too many true crime documentaries in one sitting. During such periods, the quality of humor may decline precipitously, reverting to puns and references to outdated memes. Users are advised to lower their expectations accordingly.

The Provider cannot guarantee humor delivery in environments hostile to comedy, including but not limited to: extremely formal business meetings, religious services, during medical procedures, or while someone is trying to parallel park. Some contexts demand solemnity, and the Provider acknowledges that even the funniest joke in the world has its inappropriate venues.

11. Termination

This Agreement shall remain in effect until either:

  • The User develops better taste in humor, at which point they will naturally gravitate away from the Provider's particular brand of comedy toward something more sophisticated, like puns in Latin or New Yorker cartoons that require an advanced degree to comprehend.
  • The Provider runs out of material and resorts to knock-knock jokes, at which point both parties agree it would be more merciful to end the relationship than to endure such comedic degradation.
  • The heat death of the universe renders all human endeavor, including comedy, meaningless. The Provider acknowledges that in the grand cosmic scale, all humor is temporary and ultimately inconsequential, but maintains that this makes it more precious rather than less valuable.
  • Either party blocks the other on social media, which in modern terms constitutes a complete severance of all human connection more final and irreversible than any legal document could hope to achieve.
  • The User sends the Provider a formal cease and desist letter written in crayon, which would be both legally unenforceable and aesthetically pleasing enough to earn the Provider's respect and compliance.

Upon termination, the User shall cease all engagement with the Provider's Jokes and shall undertake a solemn vow to pretend they never found them funny in the first place. This revisionist history is a time-honored tradition in the dissolution of comedic relationships and shall be respected by all parties.

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of common sense, basic human decency, and whatever mood the Provider happens to be in at the time of any dispute. The Provider acknowledges that all three of these governing principles are highly subjective and variable, making this essentially a legal document written in sand during high tide.

Any disagreements shall be settled by a dance-off, a pun contest, or trial by meme, at the Provider's discretion. The Provider reserves the right to choose whichever conflict resolution method most favors their particular skills, which is admittedly unfair but consistent with how most legal systems actually function beneath their veneer of impartiality.

The jurisdiction for all disputes arising from this Agreement shall be the Court of Public Opinion, where everyone's a judge and the sentence is always harsh. Appeals may be filed with the Supreme Court of "Let It Go Already," though the Provider notes that the backlog for cases in this court currently extends to infinity.

In the event that an actual legal dispute somehow arises from this obviously satirical document, both parties agree to take a long, hard look at their life choices and consider whether this is really the hill they want to die on. The Provider suggests that anyone considering legal action based on a joke terms of service might benefit from a hobby, a vacation, or perhaps a soothing cup of tea.
